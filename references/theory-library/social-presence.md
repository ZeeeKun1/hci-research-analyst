# Social Presence Theory

## Overview

Social Presence Theory (Short, Williams, & Christie, 1976) examines how communication media vary in their ability to convey the "sense of being with another." Central to understanding CMC (computer-mediated communication) and collaborative systems.

## Core Constructs

- **Social Presence**: The degree to which a medium conveys the feeling that communicators are socially present — that other people are "real" and "there."
- **Intimacy**: Warmth and closeness conveyed through the medium (eye contact, facial expression, voice tone).
- **Immediacy**: Psychological distance between communicators (formal vs informal language, responsiveness).

### Biocca's Three Dimensions
1. **Co-presence**: Sense of being together in a shared space.
2. **Psychological involvement**: Cognitive and emotional engagement with the other.
3. **Behavioral engagement**: Sense of being accessible and available to the other.

### Social Presence in CMC Evolution
- Text-only CMC → low social presence but surprisingly capable
- Audio/video → increased nonverbal cue transmission
- VR/avatars → embodied co-presence with new possibilities

## Key Predictions

1. Higher social presence → more natural and satisfying communication.
2. Media richness matters but users adapt — text can convey high social presence with experience.
3. Social presence cues (emoticons, response latency, turn-taking patterns) function as social signals.
4. Anonymity reduces social presence but can increase self-disclosure (paradox).
5. Social presence influences trust formation, cooperation, and persuasion.
6. Avatar design and embodiment significantly affect social presence in virtual environments.

## HCI Applications

| Context | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| Video conferencing | Design for eye contact, spatial audio, nonverbal cues |
| Social VR | Avatar fidelity, gaze behavior, proximity dynamics |
| Collaborative tools | Awareness displays, presence indicators, activity feeds |
| Online communities | Reputation systems, profile design, communication norms |
| Telemedicine | Establishing rapport and trust at distance |

## Measuring Social Presence

- **Subjective**: Social Presence Scale (Short et al.), Networked Minds Measure (Biocca et al.), Temple Presence Inventory
- **Behavioral**: Prosocial behavior, cooperation levels, mimicry, conversational turn-taking
- **Physiological**: Skin conductance synchrony (physiological coupling)

## When Findings Map to Social Presence Theory

- Users feel "alone" despite being connected → Low co-presence
- Users describe avatar-mediated interaction as "real" → High social presence
- Users trust some media more than others → Immediacy/intimacy effects
- Users adapt their communication style to the medium → Media adaptation
- Users report stronger bonds through certain interaction modes → Psychological involvement
