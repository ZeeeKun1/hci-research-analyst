# Technology Acceptance Model (TAM) / UTAUT

## Overview

TAM (Davis, 1989) and its successor UTAUT (Venkatesh et al., 2003) explain technology adoption behavior through users' beliefs and attitudes. Among the most widely applied frameworks in HCI and IS research.

## Core Constructs — TAM

- **Perceived Usefulness (PU)**: The degree to which a person believes using a system enhances job performance.
- **Perceived Ease of Use (PEOU)**: The degree to which a person believes using a system is effort-free.
- **Behavioral Intention (BI)**: Intention to use the system.
- **Actual Use**: Self-reported or measured usage behavior.

Model: PU → BI → Use; PEOU → PU; PEOU → BI. External variables (e.g., training, system features) influence PU and PEOU.

## Core Constructs — UTAUT

Four key determinants:
1. **Performance Expectancy**: Belief that the system helps achieve gains (supersedes PU + motivation).
2. **Effort Expectancy**: Degree of ease (supersedes PEOU).
3. **Social Influence**: Perception that important others believe one should use it.
4. **Facilitating Conditions**: Belief that organizational/technical infrastructure exists.

Moderators: gender, age, experience, voluntariness of use.

## Key Predictions

1. Perceived usefulness is the strongest predictor of adoption intention.
2. Perceived ease of use has both direct and indirect effects (through usefulness).
3. Social influence matters more in mandatory settings; less in voluntary adoption.
4. Facilitating conditions predict actual use but not always intention.
5. Effects change over time — experience moderates most relationships.

## When Findings Map to TAM/UTAUT

- Users mention usefulness/productivity → Performance Expectancy
- Users mention ease/difficulty of learning → Effort Expectance
- Users mention peer pressure or social norms → Social Influence
- Users mention technical barriers, support, resources → Facilitating Conditions
- Users adopt despite difficulties (or reject despite ease) → Interesting tension with theory

## Critiques & Extensions

- **Over-explained**: High R² in original studies may reflect common method variance.
- **Novelty effect**: Longitudinal studies show relationships change post-adoption.
- **TAM3**: Added computer anxiety, computer self-efficacy, playfulness.
- **UTAUT2**: Added hedonic motivation, price value, habit (for consumer contexts).
