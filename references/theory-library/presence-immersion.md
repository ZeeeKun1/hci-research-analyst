# Presence & Immersion Theory

## Overview

Presence — the subjective sensation of "being there" in a mediated environment — is central to VR/XR HCI research. Two major perspectives: presence as transportation (Slater) and presence as perceptual illusion (Lombard & Ditton).

## Core Constructs

### Slater's View (Suspension of Disbelief)
- **Place Illusion (PI)**: The sensation of being in the mediated place, despite knowing it's not real.
- **Plausibility Illusion (Psi)**: The sensation that the events occurring are really happening.
- Both are binary and automatic (like optical illusion).
- Distinguished from immersion (objective property of the system).

### Lombard & Ditton's Six Conceptualizations
1. **Presence as social richness** (medium ≈ face-to-face)
2. **Presence as realism** (medium looks/sounds real)
3. **Presence as transportation** (you are there / it is here / we are together)
4. **Presence as immersion** (being surrounded by the environment)
5. **Presence as social actor within medium** (responding to mediated entities as social actors)
6. **Presence as medium as social participant** (medium as active social agent)

## Key Predictions

1. Higher immersion (technical fidelity) → higher probability of presence, but not guaranteed.
2. Breaks in presence (BIPs) disrupt the illusion — e.g., lag, UI glitches, incongruent physics.
3. Plausibility depends on coherency of scenario, not just visual fidelity.
4. Embodiment (body ownership) enhances presence and can affect attitudes/behavior.
5. Presence correlates with engagement, enjoyment, and attitude change.

## HCI Applications

| Context | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| VR/XR design | Maximize immersion while minimizing BIPs |
| Telepresence | Design for place illusion in remote collaboration |
| Gaming | Balance immersion with gameplay needs |
| Therapy (VR exposure) | Presence as mechanism of treatment efficacy |
| Education in VR | Presence predicts learning engagement |

## Measuring Presence

- **Subjective**: ITC-SOPI (Witmer & Singer), SUS (Slater-Usoh-Steed), IPQ (Presence Questionnaire)
- **Behavioral**: Postural sway, proximity behaviors, verbal responses
- **Physiological**: Skin conductance, heart rate variability, EEG
